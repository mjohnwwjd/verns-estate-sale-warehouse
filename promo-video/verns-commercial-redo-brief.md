## Inspiration Notes

What to borrow from good thrift/resale/estate-sale marketing:

- Show real store/floor content instead of generic claims.
- Use short, punchy video for new arrivals, flash deals, and floor updates.
- Show "find of the week" style items so people understand why they should check back.
- Give viewers a clear reason to visit now: fresh inventory, markdowns, and one-of-a-kind pieces.
- Make the spot reusable in multiple formats: website hero, Facebook, Instagram Reels, and in-store screen.
- Keep it authentic. The warehouse should still feel like Vern's, not a glossy mall store.

What to avoid:

- Do not show fake guaranteed profits.
- Do not imply every item can be flipped for big money.
- Do not use copied logos, Facebook UI, EstateSales.NET UI, eBay UI, or trademarked marketplace screens.
- Do not make it look like a charity thrift store if the message is really warehouse deals and estate sale finds.
- Do not use tiny text, long paragraphs, or complicated motion.
- Do not use surreal cartoon objects or goofy animated doodles.

## Creative Direction

Working title:

**The Good Hunt at Vern's**

Alternate title:

**Worth the Stop**

Tone:

- Local, useful, slightly exciting.
- Old-school warehouse sign energy.
- Retro mid-century / antique feel, but not dusty or slow.
- Friendly enough for older customers.
- Interesting enough for resellers, collectors, decorators, and deal hunters.

Visual style:

- Realistic warehouse aisles and sale tables.
- Quick close-ups of price tags, drawers opening, tools on a table, glass catching light, framed art, lamps, and clearance tags.
- Handheld but polished, like a local commercial with good taste.
- Dark charcoal/black backgrounds, slate gray, aged cream, Vern's yellow/gold tags, small red clearance accents.
- Big readable captions.
- Real photos/video from Vern's should beat AI assets whenever available.

Music:

- Upbeat garage-blues, rockabilly, or old-school organ/guitar groove.
- Lightly gritty, not slick corporate.
- Keep voiceover readable.

Voice:

- Warm local voice.
- Male or female is fine.
- Should sound like someone inviting you to a real place, not a national ad.
- Confident, practical, friendly.
